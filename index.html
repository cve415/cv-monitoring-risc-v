The Uncore PMU Proposal

Many fond experiences in performance monitoring and observability during my years at New Relic and AppDynamics watching developers solve issues across front-end and server code. Usually, the culprit was memory bandwidth saturation, cache thrashing, or interconnect contention happening outside the core—things that used to be difficult to see until it was too late.

RISC-V's new Uncore PMU proposal (shoutout to Zhou Song and the team at Alibaba, Hangzhou) tackles exactly this blind spot. It standardizes performance monitoring for last-level cache, memory controllers, coherent interconnects, and I/O subsystems—the places where modern AI, HPC, and data center workloads actually live or die.
